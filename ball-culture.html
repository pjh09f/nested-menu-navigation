Ball Culture

Ball culture began as a radical form of self-expression and survival for Black and Latinx queer people in 1970s and 80s New York. It offered a space to walk, compete, and exist outside of a world that didn’t see or value them. Voguing — with its sharp poses and fluid movements — emerged from this scene, inspired by fashion magazines but shaped by protest, resilience, and joy. Today, ball culture continues to evolve, spotlighted in shows like Pose and celebrated globally. But its roots remain sacred: a defiant celebration of chosen family, gender creativity, and being seen.
