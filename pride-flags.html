Pride Flags

Pride flags aren’t just colorful decorations — they’re powerful symbols of identity, visibility, and community. Each flag in the photo below represents a different slice of the LGBTQ+ spectrum, telling its own story through color and design. They’ve become essential markers at marches, events, and online spaces — a way to say “I see you” without saying a word.

Rainbow Flag: The original and most recognized pride flag. It represents the full LGBTQ+ community and was designed by Gilbert Baker in 1978.

Transgender Flag: Light blue, pink, and white stripes represent trans men, trans women, and nonbinary individuals. Created by Monica Helms in 1999.

Nonbinary Flag: Yellow, white, purple, and black — each color representing a different experience of gender outside the binary.

Bisexual Flag: Pink, purple, and blue stand for same-gender attraction, attraction to multiple genders, and the overlap in between.

Progress Flag: A modern redesign that includes stripes for Black and Brown queer people, as well as trans people — pushing forward inclusion within the LGBTQ+ movement.
